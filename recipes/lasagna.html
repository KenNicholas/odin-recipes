Odin Lasagna

Home

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that consists of layers of pasta, meat sauce, and cheese. It is a hearty and comforting meal that is perfect for feeding a crowd.

The dish is typically made with ground beef or pork, but it can also be made with chicken or vegetables for a vegetarian version. The meat sauce is usually made with tomatoes, onions, garlic, and herbs, and it is simmered until it is thick and flavorful. The cheese layer is typically made with ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, and Parmesan cheese, which are mixed together to create a creamy and delicious filling. The lasagna is then baked in the oven until the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the pasta is cooked to perfection. It is often served with a side of garlic bread and a salad for a complete meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef or pork
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 ounces) tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 15 ounces ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef or pork over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  3. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the skillet and cook until the onion is translucent.
  4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer the sauce for about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Meanwhile, cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  6. In a mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, shredded mozzarella cheese, and grated Parmesan cheese.
  7. Spread a thin layer of the meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Layer 4 lasagna noodles on top of the sauce.
  8. Spread one-third of the cheese mixture over the noodles, followed by one-third of the remaining meat sauce. Repeat this layering process two more times, ending with a layer of meat sauce on top.
  9. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  10. Let the lasagna cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy!
